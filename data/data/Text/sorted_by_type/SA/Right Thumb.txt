

# Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential (Right Thumb)

This finger is regarding one’s self-correction. This potential also includes one’s internal execution ability, one’s understanding of day self, one’s capacity for self-discipline and management, one’s ability to deal with stress and making good use of these abilities to plan or shape own future. Individuals with a high intrapersonal and self management potential have a high self-esteem, a high aim in life, and are more independent in solving problems and more thoughtful.

**Type**: SA  
**%**: 0.00%  
**RC**: (0-Unlimited)

## test - Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential

1. Ability to self-correction: Absorbs data in a sponge-like manner. Environment and development after birth plays an important role in self-correction and self-management  
2. Capacity for self-discipline and management: Generally conservative and cautious. Prioritises efficiency and plays by the. Down-to-earth. Goes to the book and follows the norm  
3. Ability to deal with stress: Able to deal with stress. Conservative and strict.  
4. Internal execution ability: Able to follow clear and concise instructions and therefore requires more supervision and counseling.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential

1. Diversify and individualise learning starting from infancy.  
2. Encourage housework from a young age.  
3. Encourage positive reinforcement to build self-esteem.  
4. If young, train your child to make decision from a young age so that your child’s self-management can be strengthened.

---

## Other Suggestions

1. If young, when a child asks a question, do not scold or forbid the child from asking questions, but answer seriously instead (do research in library, use encyclopedia, on the internet etc).  
2. If young, develop a balance coordination between the left and right brain of the child so that the child will develop on logical hand thinking and deduction. A balance between the two interest in the right and left hand of the child is very beneficial to the development of the brain. Allow child to learn typing, play the piano, knitting, excel hand calligraphy.  
3. Partake mathematical calculations exercises and play number games.  
4. Read more books on science, deduction and logic.  
5. Practise diversified thinking to stimulate the ability to solve problems.  
6. Learn basics of computer language such as LOGO, BASIC and PASCAL.  
7. Practise structured logical thinking.  
8. Analyse and explain the meaning of numerals and solve mathematical problems (such as loan interest, mortgage and exponential etc.)  
9. If young, let child make a shopping list complete with types of products, quantity and price when shopping and calculate the total expenditure together with the child.  
10. Play more games that will boost thinking power such as riddles and brainstorming.  
11. If young, let child organise and discuss inter-relations among different objects.  
12. Practise using logic to uncover the contradiction in the speech of others.  
13. Buy a full set of encyclopedia and gradually nurture the habit and ability of finding information and knowledge so that the child will have a thirst for knowledge.  
14. Organise a study group or discussion group to discuss new scientific theories and discoveries, discuss mathematical terms and scientific knowledge that are not understood.  
15. Discuss with friends economical or financial views from the newspapers and the effects of them in our daily lives.  
16. Play more chess, Go, number games etc.  
17. Observe the scientific concepts and theories used in making the products in our daily life.  
18. View a matter from different perspectives and understand that there is not only one standard answer to a problem or question.
