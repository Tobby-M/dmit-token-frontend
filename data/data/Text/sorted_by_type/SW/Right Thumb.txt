# Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential (Right Thumb)

This finger is regarding one’s self-correction. This potential also includes one’s mental execution ability, one’s understanding of own self, one’s capacity for self-discipline and management, one’s ability to deal with stress and making good use of these abilities to plan or shape own future. Individuals with a high intrapersonal and self-management potential have a high self-esteem, a high aim in life, and are more independent in solving problems and more thoughtful.

**Type**: SW  
**%**: 0.00%  
**RC**: (0-Unlimited)

---

## test - Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential

1. **Ability to self-correction:** High demand on self and others. Stubborn and opinionated.  
2. **Capacity for self-discipline and management:** Good management skills and is good at analysis and planning. Emphasises order and minor details.  
3. **Ability to deal with stress:** Hard evidence and logical convincing is required for effective communication and ability to deal with stress. Shows strong loyalty once trust is obtained.  
4. **Internal execution ability:** Strong internal execution ability. Prioritises efficiency. Will go all out once a goal is set. Execution method is based on type of goal set. Little chance of compromising, thus resulting in huge stress on others.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Intrapersonal And Self-Management Potential

1. Practise listening to the opinions of others.  
2. Set short to long term goals for self and work towards them.  
3. If young, let child arrange and manage time for work and play independently.  
4. Develop the ability to make decisions starting from a young age so that self-management potential can be developed.

---

## Other Suggestions

1. If young, when a child asks a question, do not scold or forbid the child from asking questions, but answer seriously instead (do research in library, use encyclopedia, on the Internet etc.).  
2. If young, develop a balance coordination between the left and right brain of the child so that the child will develop an interest in logical thinking and deduction. A balance between the use of the right and left hand of the child is very beneficial to the development of the brain. Allow child to learn typing, play the piano, knitting, or left hand calligraphy.  
3. Practise mathematic calculation exercises and play number games.  
4. Read more books on science, deduction and logic.  
5. Practise diversified thinking to stimulate the ability to solve problems.  
6. Learn abacus or computer language such as LOGO, BASIC and PASCAL.  
7. Practise structured logical thinking.  
8. Analyse and explain the meaning of numerals and solve mathematical problems (such as loan interest, income and expenditure etc.).  
9. If young, let child make a shopping list complete with types of products, quantity and price when shopping and calculate the total expenditure together with the child.  
10. Play more games that will boost thinking power such as riddles and brainstorming.  
11. If young, let child organise and discuss inter-relations among different objects.  
12. Practise using logic to uncover the contradiction in the speech of others.  
13. Buy a full set of encyclopedia and gradually nurture the habit and ability of finding information and knowledge so that the child will have a thirst for knowledge.  
14. Organise a study group or discussion group to discuss new scientific theories and discoveries, discuss mathematical terms and scientific knowledge that are not understood.  
15. Discuss with friends economical or financial views from the newspapers and the effects of them in our daily lives.  
16. Play more chess, Go, number games etc.  
17. Observe the scientific concepts and theories used in making the products in our daily life.  
18. View a matter from different perspectives and understand that there is not only one standard answer to a problem or question.
