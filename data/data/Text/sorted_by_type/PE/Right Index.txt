
# Logical Thinking, Deduction And Reasoning Intelligence (Right Index)

The ability to grasp the concept of cause and effect, categorisation ability, ability to systematically solve problems using the concept of order and ranking, and inductive analysis. Individuals with a high logical thinking, deduction and reasoning potential tend to be more interested in numbers and scientific logical related topics. These individuals are also more systematic and follow certain procedures in problem-solving. They like to use logical thinking in learning.

**Type:** PE  
**%:** 0.00%  
**RC:** (0-Unlimited)

## test - Logical Thinking, Deduction And Reasoning Intelligence

1. **Thinking pattern:** Logical as well as sensible thinking pattern.  
2. **Logical and reasoning abilities:** Detailed, systematic and practical thinking. Strong deduction abilities and very creative.  
3. **Inductive analysis:** Good at inductive analysis. Detailed and structured in expressing ideas.  
4. **Learning pattern:** Silent for unverified learning style.  
5. **Ability to adapt:** Quick thinking and response, able to think in multiple ways, strong perseverance when dealing with difficulties.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Logical Thinking, Deduction And Reasoning Intelligence

1. Convinced with logic, reasons and proof and set down clear and concise learning objectives. Able to excel with proper guidance.  
2. If young, make sure the child understands the whole concept, cause and effect of the subject while learning.  
3. The concept of “vice-versa” should be imparted. For example, during encounters with foreigners, parents should ask “They appear as foreigners to us but at the same time, do we appear as foreigners to them as well?”  
4. Accumulate experience to build organised thinking and analytical abilities.

---

## Other Suggestions

1. If young, when a child asks a question, do not scold or forbid the child from asking questions, but answer seriously instead (do research in library, use encyclopedia, on the Internet etc.).  
2. If young, develop a balance coordination between the left and right brain of the child so that the child will develop an interest in logical thinking and deduction. A balance between the use of the right hand and left hand of the child is very beneficial to the development of the brain. Allow child to learn typing, play the piano, knitting, or left hand calligraphy.  
3. Practise mathematic calculation exercises and play number games.  
4. Read more books on science, deduction and logic.  
5. Practise diversified thinking to stimulate the ability to solve problems.  
6. Learn abacus or computer language such as LOGO, BASIC and PASCAL.  
7. Practise structured logical thinking.  
8. Analyse and explain the meaning of numerals and solve mathematical problems (such as loan interest, income and expenditure etc.).  
9. If young, let child make a shopping list complete with types of products, quantity and price when shopping and calculate the total expenditure together with the child.  
10. Play more games that will boost thinking power such as riddles and brainstorming.  
11. If young, let child organise and discuss inter-relations among different objects.  
12. Practise using logic to uncover the contradiction in the speech of others.  
13. Buy a full set of encyclopedia and gradually nurture the habit and ability of finding information and knowledge so that the child will have a thirst for knowledge.  
14. Organise a study group or discussion group to discuss new scientific theories and discoveries, discuss mathematical terms and scientific knowledge that are not understood.  
15. Discuss with friends economical or financial views from the newspapers and the effects of them in our daily lives.  
16. Play more chess, Go, number games etc.  
17. Observe the scientific concepts and theories used in making the products in our daily life.  
18. View a matter from different perspectives and understand that there is not only one standard answer to a problem or question.  
19. Observe the happenings in daily life and record down any doubts. Think about the reasons behind these happenings through observations and experiments. New problems may occur while thinking of the doubts which require further analysis and observation that merge science and nature.
