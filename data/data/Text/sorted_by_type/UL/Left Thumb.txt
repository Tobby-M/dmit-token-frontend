
# Interpersonal And Innovation Potential (Left Thumb)

The ability to interact with others, including one's interactive model, one's sensitivity towards the emotion, motives and feelings of others, one's ability to react differently to the emotions of others. Individuals with high interpersonal and innovation potential are more considerate and diplomatic. Besides that, this finger also represents one’s external execution ability such as the development and innovation of a new line of business or affair.

**Type**: UL  
**%**: 0.00%  
**RC**: (0-Unlimited)

---

## test - Interpersonal And Innovation Potential

1. **Interactive model:** Hardly initiates an interaction but friendly and easy to get along with. Thus sometime affects his/her execution ability. Enjoys group activities.  
2. **Ability to develop new affairs:** Prioritises the process rather than the outcome of developing new affairs.  
3. **Ability to deal with goals:** Sometimes, objectives may fail due to prioritising relationships but has infinite potential when a sense of responsibility or mission is present. Tends to escape from stress.  
4. **Active behaviour in relationships:** Peace-loving, kind, and resents conflict. Sensitive towards emotions, motives and feelings of others.  
5. **External character:** More difficult to execute completely but takes action actively towards work with mission.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Interpersonal And Innovation Potential

1. Try to initiate a conversation with a stranger and take part in activities interested in to get to know more friends. If young, parents should guide them to get to know new friends.  
2. Develop control of own emotion. Count to ten and take a deep breath before losing your temper.  
3. Develop ability to achieve objectives and goals (eg. Team up to achieve certain tasks).  
4. Make the goals more concrete and instilled with mission to motivate yourself to work hard towards goals.  
5. Prioritise the result or outcome over the process.  
6. Reasons for rewards and punishments should be made clear.

---

## Other Suggestions

1. Participate in group activities or games (i.e. birthday parties, religious activities [...], New Year, joining the Scout etc.)  
2. Participate in motivation camps, leadership camps etc.  
3. Develop interpersonal skills (learn how to make requests, answer, listen, praise, encourage etc.)  
4. Vise to be committee member in organisations, take opportunities to make own self heard more often.  
5. Participate in religion, charity events, voluntary work (i.e. Red Cross Society, Go Green Campaign etc.)  
6. Spend 10–20 minutes daily to practise listening skills  
7. Pay attention to the conversations among hosts and guests on the show to improve on communication skills.  
8. Take the initiative to converse with others in public.  
9. Use Index Card or categorise name cards accordingly (relatives, friends, schoolmates, business acquaintances) and keep in touch with them.  
10. Learn in groups (study group, discussion group etc.)  
11. Attend seminars from time to time and make more friends.  
12. Organise a brainstorming group and have activities accordingly from time to time.  
13. Read more books on socialisation, etiquette and etc.  
14. Participate in games or competitions that require teamwork.  
15. If young, parents should listen attentively to children after they finish school in order to understand more about their social situation in school  
16. Learn how to make full use of the strengths of every team member and be more competitive.  
17. From young, instil moral values in child so that life can be more meaningful.
