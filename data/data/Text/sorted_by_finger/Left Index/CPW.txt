

# Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential (Left Index)

The ability to convert visible things into a three-dimensional image in the brain, the ability to modify the information received and visualise it in the brain, which enhances reading and memory capacity. This potential also includes sense of spatial imagination and direction. Individuals with a high holistic imagery and spatial potential are able to solve mathematical trigonometric problems easily, able to identify roads easily and have a good sense of direction, and like to use diagrams, graphs or pictures to express themselves.

**Type**: CPW  
**%**: 0.00%  
**RC**: (0-Unlimited)

## test - Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential

1. **Way of thinking:** Multiple ways of thinking pattern. Strong imagination and strong at relating different information.  
2. **Sense of spatial imagination and direction:** Strong imagination and able to relate from one thing to another but tends to be easily distracted. Good sense of direction but may be hindered by interests.  
3. **Imaginative power:** Able to use information to diversify creativity. Open minded to new information and new trends and good at synergising this new information to produce something new.  
4. **Strategic thinking:** Strong at strategic thinking. Able to look at things with different perceptions.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential

1. Learn to mind-map, learn to focus or else you might be distracted easily.  
2. A “standard answer” will only destroy the unique innovation of the individual, especially when young.  
3. Parents should encourage child to use words or pictures to express themselves extensively.  
4. If young, prevent verbal abusing your child as that might hinder the development of creativity.  
5. Learn to materialise creative ideas.

---

## Other Suggestions

1. Participate in activities and games such as building blocks, making three dimensional models, mechanical projects etc.  
2. Participate more in games that are related to three dimensional and spatial concepts.  
3. Participate more in activities that require imagination and critical thinking such as puzzles, mazes, Rubik’s cube, association games, picture dictionary etc.  
4. Learn to find ways in the suburbs, shopping malls, parking etc. to strengthen sense of identification of direction.  
5. Learn how to make good use of space substantially at home or outdoors.  
6. Tactile stimulation of the body is encouraged during childhood.  
7. Blindfold your eyes and try to remember the decoration and position of the objects that are in the surroundings.  
8. Explore the surroundings while blindfolded.  
9. Participate in activities such as heaping sand piles at the beach.  
10. Practise to describe about the people or objects seen before and gradually try to describe more detailed information about them.  
11. Imagine certain objects in the mind and then start to change the colours of the objects.  
12. If young, allow children to put different objects into the storage room or car boot etc.  
13. Frequently visit the science center, art exhibitions, museum etc.  
14. Design some graphics and patterns by using computer software.  
15. Take part in sculpture, photography, painting, graphic design and other visual arts classes.  
16. Use pictures, symbols, diagrams, flow charts etc. to make presentations.  
17. Create a three-dimensional model based on own creativity and imagination.  
18. Learn to use the technique of “mind mapping”.  
19. Play chess, Go, or learn abacus to strengthen imagination and spatial sense.  
20. Participate in linguistic programmes such as narration of a story, essay and so on, (reduce watching TV and playing electronic games) to stimulate active thinking and imagination.  
21. Participate in activities that require imagination and creativity: acting a part of a play, photography, painting, sculpture, making models such as LEGO or Word Puzzle.  
22. Ability that involves space: Learn to make three-dimensional models, read books on interior or exterior design.  
23. Ability to improve sense of direction: Learn how to go from one place to another (city, village, jungle or inside a building).
