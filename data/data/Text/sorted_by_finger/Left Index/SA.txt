# Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential (Left Index)

The ability to convert visible things into a three-dimensional image in the brain, the ability to modify the information received and visualise it in the brain, which enhances reading and memory capacity. This potential also includes sense of spatial imagination and direction. Individuals with a high holistic imagery and spatial potential are able to solve mathematical trigonometric problems easily, able to identify roads easily and have a good sense of direction, and like to use diagrams, graphs or pictures to express themselves.

## test - Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential

1. Way of thinking: Simple and innocent way of thinking, dislikes complicated matters, stubborn and resents changes.  
2. Sense of spatial imagination and direction: Weak sense of spatial imagination and direction. Not good at grasping abstract concepts.  
3. Imaginative thinking: Limited to realistic and safe concepts due to the placing of importance in formulas and steps and quite unable to take risks  
4. Strategic thinking: Takes things step by step and must be given procedures and plans.

---

## Specific Suggestions To Improve test - Holistic Imagery And Spatial Potential

1. Practise listening to opinions of others. Broaden perception by reading more books to increase ability to accept the opinions of others.  
2. Take part in more co-curricular activities such as theme-based museum exhibitions and so on as it combines knowledge and fun.  
3. Build self-expectation and self-esteem.  
4. Prevent verbally abusing your child as that might hinder the development of creativity  
5. If young, parents should encourage child to use words or pictures to express themselves.  
6. Learn to look at things with different perceptions and to express different creative ideas.

## Other Suggestions

1. Participate in activities and games such as building blocks, making three dimensional models, mechanical projects etc.  
2. Participate more in games that are related to three dimensional and spatial concepts.  
3. Participate more in activities that require imagination and critical thinking such as puzzles, mazes, Rubik’s cube, association games, picture dictionary etc.  
4. Learn to find ways in the suburbs, shopping malls, parking, etc. to strengthen sense of identification of direction.  
5. Learn how to make good use of space substantially at home or outdoors  
6. Tactile stimulation of the body is encouraged during childhood.  
7. Blindfold your eyes and try to remember the decoration and location of the objects that are in the surrounding.  
8. Explore the surroundings while blindfolded.  
9. Participate in activities such as heaping sand piles at the beach.  
10. Practise to describe about the people or objects seen before and gradually try to describe more detailed information about them.  
11. Imagine certain objects in the mind and then start to change the colours of the objects.  
12. If young, allow children to put different objects into the storage room or car boot etc.  
13. Frequently visit the science center, art exhibitions, museums etc.  
14. Design some graphics and patterns by using computer software.  
15. Take part in sculpture, photography, painting, graphic design and other visual arts classes.  
16. Use pictures, symbols, diagrams, flow charts etc. to make / create a three-dimensional model based on own creativity and imagination.  
17. Learn to use the technique of “mind mapping”.  
18. Play chess, Go, or learn abacus to strengthen imagination and spatial sense.  
19. Participate in linguistic programmes such as narration of a story, essay and so on, [reduce watching TV and playing electronic games] to stimulate active thinking and imagination.  
20. Participate in activities that require imagination and creativity; creating a logo for e.g. photography, jewellery, sculpture, making models such as LEGO or WORD PUZZLE.  
21. Ability that involves space: Learn to make three-dimensional models, read books on interior or exterior design.  
22. Ability to improve sense of direction: Learn how to go from one place to another (city, village, jungle or inside a building).  
23. Ability to expand visual memory and space-memory. Build your own map and room arrangement.
